# How a Supreme Court Definition Can Erase 96.5% of India's Oldest Mountains

**Author:** Rushikesh Jadhav
**Published:** December 24, 2025

A geospatial analysis of the November 2025 Supreme Court of India ruling that redefined the Aravalli Hills, reducing protected area from 267,009 km² to 9,224 km² — a 96.5% loss.

Live article: [aravali_article/index.html](aravali_article/index.html)

## Methodology Summary

- **Old definition baseline:** terrain above 200m elevation (267,009 km²)
- **New court definition:** hills with ≥100m topographic prominence (calculated over a 2km radius focal window), where qualifying hills within 500m of each other form a protected "range" (9,224 km²)
- **Prominence calculation:** focal minimum subtracted from pixel elevation across a 2km circular window
- **Clustering:** morphological dilation at 500m radius + connected component labeling
- **DEM resolution:** 30m (COP30)

Full methodology is described in the article's methodology section and in `Aravali Study Methodology.docx`.
